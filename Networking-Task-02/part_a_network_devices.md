# Part A — Network Devices

**Submitted by:** Sanjana
**Date:** 06 June 2026

---

### 1. Router
**Purpose:** A router connects your home network to the internet and directs data to the right device.

**How it works:** It reads the destination IP address of every data packet and decides the best path to send it — either to a device inside your network or out to the internet.

**Real-world usage:** The Wi-Fi box at home that connects all your devices to the internet. My router's IP is `192.168.1.1`.

---

### 2. Switch
**Purpose:** A switch connects multiple devices within the same local network (LAN) and sends data only to the device it's meant for.

**How it works:** It uses MAC addresses to identify devices and forwards data directly to the correct one — unlike a hub which sends to everyone.

**Real-world usage:** In offices where many computers, printers and servers are connected together through a switch.

---

### 3. Hub
**Purpose:** A hub connects multiple devices in a network but sends data to all devices at once regardless of who it's meant for.

**How it works:** When it receives data it simply broadcasts it to every connected device and each device checks if the data is for them.

**Real-world usage:** Mostly outdated now — switches have replaced hubs because hubs waste bandwidth and are less secure.

---

### 4. Access Point
**Purpose:** An access point allows wireless devices to connect to a wired network.

**How it works:** It acts as a bridge between Wi-Fi devices and the wired network by transmitting and receiving wireless signals.

**Real-world usage:** In large offices or campuses where multiple access points are placed to provide Wi-Fi coverage across a large area.

---

### 5. Firewall
**Purpose:** A firewall monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic to protect your network from threats.

**How it works:** It checks every data packet against a set of security rules and blocks anything that looks suspicious or unauthorised.

**Real-world usage:** Companies use firewalls to block hackers from accessing internal systems. Windows also has a built-in firewall on your PC.

---

### 6. Modem
**Purpose:** A modem connects your home to your Internet Service Provider (ISP) and converts the ISP's signal into a format your devices can use.

**How it works:** It modulates and demodulates signals — converting digital data from your devices into signals that can travel through phone lines or cables, and vice versa.

**Real-world usage:** The device your ISP provides that brings internet into your home. It usually connects directly to your router.
